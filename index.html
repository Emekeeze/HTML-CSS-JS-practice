Polygon overlay is a powerful GIS operation that combines multiple polygon layers to derive new information and analyze spatial relationships. Here are two relevant examples where it is appropriate to apply polygon overlay in GIS operations: Land Use Planning: In land use planning, polygon overlay is commonly used to assess the compatibility and suitability of different land uses. By overlaying various polygon layers representing land parcels, zoning regulations, and environmental constraints, planners can make informed decisions about land development and conservation. For example: Data Collection: Collect spatial data including land parcel boundaries, zoning regulations, and environmental constraint boundaries. Import the data into GIS.

Achieving high accuracy involves ensuring that the measurements or values closely align with the true or accepted values through rigorous data collection methods, quality control processes, and validation against ground truth or reference data. Precision, on the other hand, primarily relates to the level of detail or resolution in the dataset.Therefore, it is crucial to consider both precision and accuracy when assessing data quality in GIS. While high precision can enhance the level of detail, high accuracy is necessary to ensure that the data reliably represents the real-world phenomena it aims to capture.
